Our plans are structured using COM-B, a behavioral science framework developed by researchers at University College London. Over 15+ years, it has demonstrated significant success in promoting exercise and physical activity, improving diet, enhancing medication adherence and reducing/ending unhealthy habits such as smoking. 

Simply expressed, COM-B holds that changing behaviors requires three factors working together — Capability, Opportunity, and Motivation. 

Importantly, COM-B is not simply a tool for persuading someone to change. It's a tool designed to support closing knowledge gaps, reducing everyday friction, and building the motivation to follow through — so new habits hold long after the plan ends.
