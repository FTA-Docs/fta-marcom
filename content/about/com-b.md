Our plans are structured using COM-B, a behavioral science framework developed by researchers at University College London. Over 15+ years, it has demonstrated significant success in promoting exercise and physical activity, improving diet, enhancing medication adherence and in smoking cessation. 

COM-B holds that changing behaviors requires three factors working together — Capability, Opportunity, and Motivation. It's not a tool for persuading someone to change. It's a tool designed to close knowledge gaps, reduce everyday friction and build the motivation to follow through — so new habits hold long after the plan ends.
